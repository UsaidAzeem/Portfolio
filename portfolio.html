Projects

My FYP – DIS-EXT (2026)

Large-scale social media platforms such as Twitter and Facebook have become prominent spaces for individuals and groups to express opinions, beliefs, and ideological standpoints. Due to their scale, openness, and real-time nature, monitoring extremist discourse has become highly complex.

DIS-EXT (Discourse Intelligence on Extremism) is a system I developed using NLP to analyze publicly available Twitter data. It performs high-granularity sentiment analysis, contextual filtering, and historical user profiling to detect patterns of ideological alignment over time. This project focuses on providing structured insights into public discourse beyond single-post analysis, capturing long-term behavioral tendencies of users.

Industrial Projects – Attar Industrial Area, Pakistan (2026)

Multi-Camera Surveillance System: Designed a cost-effective, round-robin surveillance system for unauthorized disturbances. By distributing computation across multiple client PCs and using threaded processing, the system detects anomalies efficiently without expensive hardware.

High-Speed Anomaly Detection for Paper Production: Developed a factory-level defect detection system using a $300 industrial camera and a Raspberry Pi instead of typical $2,000 setups. The system leverages classical computer vision techniques (thresholding and shape detection) to identify defects in real-time, minimizing losses and maintaining product quality.

These projects emphasize reducing processing cost while maintaining performance, enabling scalable industrial monitoring solutions.
